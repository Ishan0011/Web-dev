Seasons of India

Div

India is a country with different seasons. Each season has its own weather, colours, smells, sounds and living creatures. The five main seasons of India are Summer, Rainy, Autumn, Pre-Winter and Winter. These seasons bring changes in nature and affect plants, animals and human life.

Summer is the hottest season of the year. During summer, the environment looks light green, yellow and brown because many leaves dry up. The sky is bright blue and clear. Fruits like mango and watermelon are seen in yellow and red colours. The air smells dry and hot. Leaves, bark and stems feel dry and rough. Animals and birds rest in shade to protect themselves from heat. We hear sounds of birds, insects and hot winds in nature. The Rainy season brings rainfall and freshness. Trees and plants become dark green and healthy. The sky looks grey or dark blue with clouds. The smell of wet soil fills the air and feels pleasant. Leaves and soil feel wet and soft. Frogs, peacocks and insects are commonly seen during this season. The sound of raindrops, thunder and croaking frogs can be heard everywhere. Span Autumn is a season when leaves begin to fall. The environment shows yellow, brown and light green colours. The sky is clear and blue. Fruits and seeds appear yellow and dry. The weather is mild and pleasant. Leaves feel dry and light. Birds and small animals like squirrels are active. The sound of dry leaves falling and birds chirping can be heard. Pre-Winter is the season before winter begins. The weather becomes cool and dry. Plants remain green, and grains and vegetables grow well. The sky looks pale blue. The air smells fresh and cool. Leaves and stems feel cool to touch. Birds start migrating, and gentle wind sounds are heard.Winter is the coldest season. The environment appears dark green and brown. The sky is pale blue or grey. Green vegetables and dry fruits are common in this season. The air smells cold and fresh. Leaves and bark feel cold and hard. Animals try to stay warm, and insects are fewer. Cold winds and soft bird sounds are heard in nature.

Conclusion

In conclusion, each season of India is unique and beautiful. Seasons help plants grow, animals survive and make nature balanced. They also teach us how nature changes throughout the year.
